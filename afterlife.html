afterlife

2d&3d/blender/illustrator/mythology

This is an project that delves into the concept of the six realms of existence in Buddhism. The six realms, including Devas, Asura, Manusya, Tiryagyoni, Preta, and Naraka, are brought to life through a collection of twelve pieces. To offer a multi-dimensional experience to the audience, the realms are represented in both 2D and 3D. Each of the six realms features a unique character, with my face or hands, along with distinctive accessories and backgrounds. Overall, this project is a captivating exploration of a complex philosophical concept that is sure to engage and intrigue viewers.

According to Buddhism, there are six realms of existence: Devas (Gods), Asura (Demi-gods), Manusya (Humans), Tiryagyoni (Animals), Preta (Ghosts), and Naraka (Hell). Gods are the highest. They live in a happy and peaceful place called heaven, where there is no suffering, sadness, or negative emotions. To be gods in the afterlife, people must have a loving heart, treat nice to others, share things, and do good deeds without thinking about receiving them back. In contrast, hell is the most awful place to be reborn. People doing evil and bad things, such as committing a crime, being ungrateful, and having inappropriate thoughts, would be punished and suffer in hell in the afterlife.
